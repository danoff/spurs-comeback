How the Model Works

The Spurs need 4 more wins before the Knicks reach 2. Per-game win probability comes from a log-odds model anchored to the historical prior; the series probability is then computed two ways:

  • Exact Enumeration (default): sums all 25=32 win/loss paths through Games 3–7, stopping each path the instant a team clinches. Instant, deterministic, zero-dependency.
  • Monte Carlo + Beta prior: simulates the series with a Gaussian-copula correlation (ρ) between games, then forms a Beta(5.5, 34.5) posterior — a real Bayesian shrinkage toward the 13.5% prior that also yields a 95% credible interval and a live convergence chart. Seed is exposed for reproducibility.

Levers are Spurs-controlled process variables with units, each mapped to a fixed log-odds shift: logit(p_game) = logit(prior) + Σ β·lever. With every lever at zero the model returns the historical base rate by construction.
