San Antonio Spurs: 0-2 Series Deficit

A Bayesian hierarchical analysis of San Antonio's playoff statistics — trained on all 20 postseason games.

Plain English

The Spurs win when they move the ball (~27 assists), rebound (~50 boards), and shoot efficiently (~48% FG) — against the Knicks they are averaging 19 assists and 42% shooting, both well below their winning profile.

Based on their Finals performance, the model puts their comeback probability near zero; they would need to play at roughly their best playoff level to have a realistic chance — and the remaining schedule runs three of five games in New York.

Model

Bayesian hierarchical logistic regression with two partial-pooling groups: the NYK Finals (n=2) and all other 2026 playoff games (n=18).

Fit via adaptive Metropolis-Hastings, 10,000 post-warmup draws. Predictors: FG%, rebounds, assists, steals, blocks, turnovers, home/away indicator — all standardised.

The wide credible intervals are not a modeling weakness. They honestly reflect irreducible uncertainty from n=2 in the focal group. A well-calibrated Bayesian model should produce wide intervals when data is sparse.

Series Combinatorics

From 0-2, NYK needs 2 more wins; SAS needs 4. Remaining schedule maps to exact location-aware paths:

  • Sweep: P_A · P_A · P_H · P_A
  • Lose G3: (1-P_A)·P_A·P_H·P_A·P_H
  • Lose G4: P_A·(1-P_A)·P_H·P_A·P_H
  • Lose G5: P_A·P_A·(1-P_H)·P_A·P_H
  • Lose G6: P_A·P_A·P_H·(1-P_A)·P_H

P_H and P_A are posterior distributions, not point estimates. The histogram above shows the resulting series probability across all simulated draw pairs.
